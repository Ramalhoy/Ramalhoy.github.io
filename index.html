I'm based in Vitória (Brazil). My practice centers around econometric analysis, statistical modeling, and data visualization. I'm also very proficient with technical writing in four languages (PT, EN, ES, FR). The following is a selection of past, present, and upcoming projects. I welcome messages through email. You can also find me on GitHub.

Projects

Trade Exposure and Political Polarization: Evidence from the Brazilian Case (ONGOING) — Masters dissertation project. It aims to assess the impact of trade shocks on the polarization of electoral behaviour in Brazil at the microregional level. Huge chunks of data, crossreferences, joins and translation of theoretical concepts into mathematics. Should be done by the end of the year.

TFP and Public Expenditures in Brazilian States (2003–2018) — This project is my undergraduate thesis. I calculated the Total Factor Productivity (TFP) for the 26 Brazilian states over 2003–2018 and evaluated the impact of disaggregated public expenditures (by government function) on those productivity levels, using a two-way fixed effects panel data approach. Advisor: Prof. Dr. Edson Zambon Monte. You can read the paper in full here. This article was also presented at the conference Encontro de Economia do Sudeste e do Espírito Santo - 2023.

VAR model for financial time series — My very first contact with applied econometrics. This project investigates the degree of interdependence between the S&P 500 (SP500), the main US equity index, and the IBOVESPA, the main Brazilian stock market index, using a VAR model. It's a very simple exercise in technical terms from which I learned a lot, since my advisor at the time (Prof. Dr. Edson Zambon) guided me through both cross-section and time series econometrics in a period where I only had prior knowledge of a Stats 101 class. A lesson in consistency and hard work despite lack of background.
